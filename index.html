Realistic Pong

by: Miller Watson

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I thought that regular Pong had a few flaws with it that made the gameplay rather simple, which is understandable for the time that it was made. With the tools at my disposal, I set out to create a game complete with air resistance, velocity, and friction. This game stuck out to me as I had always loved 2-player local games.

2. What I Learned

I learned that some of my hitboxes were inaccurate and had to be changes. In addition, I also learned that the game went by too fast. It used to go to three points, but now it goes to ten. In addition, they also helped me discover some physics bugs.

3. Architecture of My Game

The paddles have one "player" class. This class interacts with the "ball" class through the form of collisions; when the paddle hits the ball, the ball's direction is reversed and the paddle is pushed back. In addition, I used the "Graphics" class to generate all of my graphics for this. Because the class isn't very complicated, I could not use images for the paddles, but they still look good and do their job.

4. Technical Implementation

The player class has a collision box and a velocity value for both the x and y. Along with the ball's collision box and its velocities, these values interact with each other so that when the collision boxes touch, both the player and ball are pushed back, and their respective velocities are implemented onto the opposite object.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was debugging the physics. Besides the issues with the logic of the physics, often times the paddle would not hit the ball correctly and pass through it or not even interact with it. However, with rigorous user testing, I solved the problems one at a time and slowly worked my way towards bug-free code.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would love to have better menu/end states for my game. In addition, I would like to make the physics a little more realistic with the addition of spin and actual physics data for the collisions. Lastly, I would make the controls a little more intuitive.
